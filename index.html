THE LEGACY

RACING HISTORY

1971

Celica Born

Toyota launches the first-generation Celica, establishing a sports car dynasty that will last 35 years and seven generations.

1984

MR2 Debuts

Japan's first mass-production mid-engine, rear-wheel-drive sports car. The AW11 MR2 stuns the world with supercar handling at everyday prices.

1993

WRC Domination Begins

Toyota Celica GT-Four wins its first WRC Manufacturers' Championship. The 3S-GTE engine becomes legendary in motorsport circles worldwide.

1998

Supra MK4 Legacy

The A80 Supra with the legendary 2JZ-GTE engine becomes an icon of JDM tuning culture, capable of 1000+ HP with modifications.

2019

Supra Returns

The A90 GR Supra revives the legend after a 21-year absence. Toyota Gazoo Racing brings back the icon with BMW partnership and new turbocharged power.

2022

GR Corolla Unleashed

Rally-derived hot hatch takes the world by storm. GR-FOUR AWD and 300hp from a 1.6L three-cylinder engine proves big things come in small packages.

2025

GR Lineup Evolves

Toyota Gazoo Racing expands its performance arsenal. The GR Supra receives updates, while new GR models push the boundaries of accessible performance.
